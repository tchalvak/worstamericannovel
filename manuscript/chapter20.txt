# The End

Jeb was lying on the hardwood floor.  Curled up in pain, his fingers losing their strength, the blood kept coming out.  He couldn't hold it in.  It kept on slipping out of his grasp, and he was forgetting why it was important.  Pain.  That usually meant something.


The pile of ceramic tiles on the counter fell off their perch at a kick to the counter's base.  They shattered, or bounced, or skid, pure white becoming dotted and smeared in browns and reds.  Eventually, the room was was all still, a mosaic of life and clay.
